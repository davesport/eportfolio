Introduction

Planning, management, marketing and advocacy are part of the librarian's everyday work repsonsibilities. Each step is separate. "Planning" refers to preparing and outlining the steps of a library project, and the work required to complete it. "Management" refers to Together, these four steps combine to connect library staff to their visitors, and encourage them to use their library's resources. These four actions are often completed among library staff together, working as a group. In the absence of a complete library staff, other educators and staff members have a stake in this four-step visitor engagement process.

As a student, I learned how to plan, manage, market and advocate for various library programs and services. As a library assistant, I performed each of these actions on a regular basis. Between school and work, I have greatly improved my understanding and experience with these four tasks, and see them as a larger process, with an the end goal of motivating visitors to reach out and grab the information and services they need. In the LibLearnX conference, I learned that librarians still discuss ways to improve at these tasks, even with years of experience.
