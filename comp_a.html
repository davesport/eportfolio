Introduction

There are many differences between a code of ethics, values and foundational principles. The first, "ethic," means: "a set of moral principles" ( Merriam-Webster ). A "value" is "something (such as a principle or quality) intrinsically valuable or desirable" ( Merriam-Webster ). The term "moral" means "concerning or relating to what is right and wrong in human behavior" ( Merriam-Webster ).
