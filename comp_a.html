Introduction

There are many differences between a code of ethics, values and foundational principles.

  • The first, "ethic," means: "a set of moral principles" ( Merriam-Webster ).
  • Second, a "value" is "something (such as a principle or quality) intrinsically valuable or desirable" ( Merriam-Webster ).
  • And third, the term "moral" means "concerning or relating to what is right and wrong in human behavior" ( Merriam-Webster ).

The Reference Librarian

The reference librarian in particular accomplishes their daily tasks with many values in mind. The code of ethics, values and foundational pricinples of reference librarianship will guide its professionals as they look toward the future.

The reference librarian's code of ethics includes: objectivity in searching and presenting information, acceptance of patrons' questions without judgment, and confidentiality between librarian and patron.

The reference librarian's values include high-quality patron service that welcomes and encourages patrons to continue seeking information, assistance and service that includes information literacy tactics, and planning that pushes forward the way that information is found, stored, and shared with others.

The reference librarian's morals include: open access to information, and the community member's right to information.
